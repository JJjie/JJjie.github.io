Recent Research

  • Electricity-theft Detection

    Electricity theft, the behavior that involves users conducting illegal operations on electrical meters to avoid individual electricity bills, is a common phenomenon in the developing countries. Considering its harmfulness to both power grids and the public, substantial effort has been expended to prevent or detect these behaviors.

    To explore the patterns of electricity theft hidden in the large-scale users, we conduct a deep study on the multi-source data: in addition to users’ electricity usage records, we analyze user behaviors by means of regional factors (non-technical loss) and climatic factors (temperature) in the corresponding transformer area. Our results unearth several interesting patterns: electricity thieves are likely to consume much more electrical power than normal users, especially under extremely high or low temperatures. Motivated by these empirical observations, a novel hierarchical framework is designed for identifying electricity thieves. (Hu et al., WWW'20)

  • Revisiting Time Series Modeling via Graph

    Time series modeling has attracted extensive research efforts; however, achieving both reliable efficiency and interpretability from a unified model still remains a challenging problem. Among the literature, many discretizing-sequence methods (e.g., shapelet, SAX word, segment clustering, etc.) try to capture several typical patterns, or saying state, underpinning the observed data; they offer interpretable and explanatory insights in the time series classification tasks. However, most existing studies ignore the relations among these states, which cause the evolution of time series.

    In our works, we propose to extract time-aware shapelets by designing a two-level timing factor, and uniform several discretizing-sequence representations into states. Moreover, we define and construct the evolutionary state graph, which present how time-series states evolve over time and their evolving relations. Based on that, we propose to incorporate the graph information into the time series representations by graph embedding algorithms and graph neural networks. ( Cheng et al., AAAI'20) [Project Homepage]
